Sustainability at Northeastern
With a population of over 22,000 students, day-to-day activities on Northeastern University's campus result in copious amounts of waste production and energy use. As faculty, students and staff preoccupied with collegiate happenings, it's easy to ignore our own contributions to waste on campus. Fortunately, our facilities team and university policies work diligently to minimize waste and create disposal procedures that promote a circular consumption environment.

The Climate Justice and Sustainability Hub, formally the Office of Sustainability, was launched in April 2021 to reflect Northeastern’s intention to engage, implement, and put research into action on climate justice, sustainability, and resilience across our global network. Our website will work in tangent with the Hub, to provide additional information for staff, faculty, students and the entire Northeastern community.

GHG Emissions at Northeastern
Northeastern University is on track to meet our commitment of 80% reduction in Metric Tons of Carbon Equivalent (MTCO2e) emissions from 2005 levels by 2050 on a gross square foot (gsf) basis of facilities-controlled buildings and transportation. Facilities Management is deeply committed to achieving these goals.
Composting at Northeastern
Northeastern recognizes the importance of composting for waste management. Facilities has collaborated for over a decade with Northeastern Dining Services to fully integrate composting and waste reduction in the main dining areas on the Boston Campus.
Recycling at Northeastern
Waste and recycling at Northeastern seeks to limit our environmental toll, reduce waste production, and maintain a simple and effective waste retrieval process to reflect university wide commitment. To help achieve this, Northeastern has dilligently tracked waste production on campus and uses a multi-stream recycling system.
